BINARY

An e-commerce for webinar.

Make, Promote, and Join Webinars

During the COVID-19 pandemic, many online seminars or webinars were held. Hundreds were created, published, and attended every week. How easy would it have been to find or promote one on a single platform?

What I've Learned

My team and I conducted extensive testing on this project. In addition to A/B testing, we also performed usability testing and contextual inquiry. Through this experience, I learned that the design process is iterative, where every piece of feedback can lead to a new phase of design thinking. For example, when a participant mentioned the difficulty of managing webinar payments and registration, our team had to revisit the definition stage. We needed to redefine the problems within the webinar registration process and ultimately proposed providing webinar registration with e-wallet-based payments and simplified registrant management for the webinar provider.

We had to iterate and refine our approach repeatedly, primarily to ensure that the process through BINARY wouldn't be more complex than existing webinar registration flows. Our research indicated that most people register for webinars through simple Google Forms. However, because we were also addressing the challenge of advertising the webinar itself, we focused on seamlessly categorizing and filtering webinars based on our prospective users' needs. Therefore, I also learned that accurately defining the problem is crucial before proposing a solution.

My Roles

UI/UX Designer. This was my first experience with the full UI/UX design thinking process.
