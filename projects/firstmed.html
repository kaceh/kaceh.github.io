FIRSTMED

Get the first aid you're looking for.

No More Overthinking

FIRSTMED allows user to check their symptoms and gives them information about possible sicknesses and what they need to do to address them during the early stages.

What I've Learned

I learned to design for specific conditions, not just user groups. For instance, understanding how people think, react, and what information they seek when experiencing physical discomfort or alarm. This empathic process alone informed several key design decisions for FIRSTMED.

I needed to reconsider the size, prominence, and spacing of touchpoints on the screen. During panic situations, people are prone to errors, so components must be clearly communicated to prevent additional stress. Short blocks of text are also preferable, enabling users to scan information quickly and minimizing misunderstandings.

My Roles

As the Frontend Developer & UI/UX Designer. As this was an individual project, I worked on everything on my own, starting from identifying the problem, did the design thinking process, and code the web myself.
